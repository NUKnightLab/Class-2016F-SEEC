  • The Problem

    • Let's walk through your average scenario exploring data. First, you might search for a term on FRED or another economic database. The result is a graph that you are forced to interpret by yourself. If you're analytically savvy, you might know how to interpret the graph. But overall, your search starts and ends with one graph, lacking an immense amount of context for anything that has contributed to the shape of that graph.

    But what if there was a tool that allowed you to discover contextual clues about the data you were looking at? Meet Joint.ly.

    What it is

    Joint.ly is a tool that allows you to explore correlations between related sets of data. Once you search for a set of data, recommended datasets will appear underneath your search; datasets with the highest number of correlations will appear near the top and datasets with fewer number of correlations descending near the bottom of the recommended list.

    Tips & tricks

    1. Keep it short. We recommend not having more than 20 slides for a reader to click through.
    2. Pick stories that have a strong chronological narrative. It does not work well for stories that need to jump around in the timeline.
    3. Write each event as a part of a larger narrative.
